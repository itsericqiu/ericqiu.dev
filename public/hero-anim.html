Hero Name.

Three approaches to transitioning the letter-seg colour on "Eric". Fade is live in production. Scan and Type are prototypes — switch modes and hit Next to compare.

ENGINEERING — NEW YORK

Eric Qiu.

Smooth fade

CSS transition: color 0.8s ease-in-out on the span. The browser interpolates between the old and new colour values smoothly. Ambient and continuous — you notice it changed but not the change happening. Current production implementation.

Pro — ambient, never competes with reading; pure CSS in production
Con — easy to miss entirely; transition passes through intermediate hues
Verdict — safe, tasteful, low drama. Works for an editorial portfolio.

Diagonal scan

A gradient boundary sweeps left-to-right at 82° (near-horizontal, slight diagonal lean). Both old and new colours are simultaneously visible on the letterforms during the 400ms wipe. Uses @property --scan-pos for an animatable gradient position on a background-clip: text span.

Pro — the transition has direction and intention; most "designed"
Con — JS must track old/new colour; shimmer risk at fast speeds
Verdict — highest perceived craft. Worth it if the timing is right.

Type-based

Each letter is individually wrapped in a span. On transition, colour updates stagger left-to-right at 40ms intervals with a 55ms CSS transition per letter. The word repaints in ~150ms — fast enough to avoid the typewriter cliché, directional enough to feel intentional.

Pro — fits mono/terminal DNA; no @property needed; simple implementation
Con — requires JS to wrap letters; less dramatic on short words
Verdict — surprisingly good at 1.5s speed. Feels like a status update.
